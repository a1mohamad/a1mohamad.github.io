How I Approach Machine Learning

Machine Learning research is more than just fitting models; it is the science of extracting signal from noise. My process begins with Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), where I investigate statistical distributions, identify collinearity, and handle anomalies. I believe that a model is only as good as the data that feeds it.

Whether I am working on Computer Vision tasks using TensorFlow/Keras or Natural Language Processing with PyTorch and then move to Game-Changer Transformers, I follow a rigorous pipeline: Dataset creation, Preprocessing, feature engineering, strategic cross-validation, hyperparameter optimization, and residual diagnosis.

My research spans from beginner-level benchmarks like the Titanic or California Housing to complex Kaggle challenges such as Cassava Leaf Disease and Quora Question Pairs. I leverage a diverse toolkit depending on the problem constraints:

PyTorch TensorFlow/Keras Transformers (HuggingFace) XGBoost LightGBM Scikit-Learn CatBoost NLTK/Spacy Pandas Numpy Matplotlib
